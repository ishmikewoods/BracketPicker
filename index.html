How Picks Are Made

Seed Weighting Core

Non-linear power ratings favor top 4 seeds, reflecting their historically dominant tournament performance.

Seeds 1–4: power gaps of 7–8 per seed
Seeds 5–8: power gaps of 4–5 per seed
Seeds 9–16: power gaps of 3 per seed
Cap: 90% max / 10% min

Blue Blood Bonus Modifier

Historically elite programs get a small edge reflecting tournament DNA.

Tier 1 (+4%): Duke, Kansas, UConn, Kentucky, North Carolina, UCLA
Tier 2 (+2%): Gonzaga, Villanova, Michigan St, Florida, Houston

Home Court Modifier

1-seeds play their first two games in their home region, giving them a crowd advantage.

Bonus: +3% for 1-seeds in R64 and R32

Conference Strength Modifier

Power conference teams get an edge against mid-major opponents.

Bonus: +3% for power conf vs non-power
Power conferences: ACC, Big Ten, Big 12, SEC, Big East

Rivalry Volatility Modifier

When rivals meet, odds flatten toward 50/50. Rivalry games are inherently unpredictable.

Intense (e.g. Duke/UNC): 35% flatten
Strong (e.g. Purdue/Illinois): 20% flatten
Moderate (e.g. Kansas/Nebraska): 10% flatten

Round Scaling Modifier

Later rounds flatten odds — every surviving team is dangerous.

R64: 0% • R32: 3% • Sweet 16: 6%
Elite 8: 10% • Final Four: 13% • Championship: 16%

Cinderella Rule Constraint

Forces realistic deep underdog runs, matching historical patterns.

Minimum 2 Cinderellas (seeds 11–14) must reach the Sweet 16

Upset Quota Constraint

Targets a realistic number of first-round upsets.

Target: ~8 first-round upsets (tolerance of 3)

Play-in Games Core

First Four matchups are decided by coin flip since both teams share the same seed.

Probability: 50/50 for all play-in games
